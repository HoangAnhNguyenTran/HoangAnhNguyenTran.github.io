## Overall Evaluation

### 1. Learning and Working Environment

The FCAJ internship environment felt professional, open, and clearly oriented toward practical capability building. The weekly structure kept the learning journey coherent while still leaving enough room for self-driven exploration beyond minimum requirements.

### 2. Mentor and Program Support

What I valued most was focused and timely support. Mentors did not hand-hold; instead, they guided us on how to find references, validate outputs, and frame technical problems correctly. The program coordination team also maintained good communication rhythm and helped interns stay on track.

### 3. Relevance to My Major

The internship content aligned well with my Software Engineering and cloud-infrastructure direction. Topics from university such as networking, databases, operating systems, and security became much clearer when applied to real deployment scenarios on AWS.

### 4. Learning and Skill Development Opportunities

The program improved both hard skills and soft skills. Technically, I learned how to connect AWS services into an architecture with clear goals. Professionally, I improved in communication, teamwork, documentation quality, and explaining technical content in a more structured way.

### 5. Community and Team Spirit

Another major positive point was the FCAJ community spirit. Learning with peers who share similar Cloud/AI goals made the internship more motivating and less isolated. Workshops and technical sharing sessions also broadened perspective beyond individual tasks.

## Additional Questions

### What I Found Most Satisfying

What satisfied me most was the opportunity to work on cloud problems close to real deployment contexts instead of isolated labs only. Linking weekly learning outcomes to a team product and final workshop made the experience practical and goal-oriented.

### What I Hope to Improve in Future Cohorts

I hope the program can include more structured intermediate review milestones for both project and workshop tracks so interns receive earlier feedback. Optional deep-dive sessions on automation, security, and production-incident simulation would also add significant value.

### Would I Recommend This Program?

Yes. I would recommend FCAJ to students interested in Cloud Computing, DevOps, or infrastructure engineering because the program offers a clear roadmap, practical content, and a supportive learning-by-doing environment.

## Suggestions and Expectations

- Add optional advanced tracks focused on automation, security, and production-style scenario practice.
- Increase the number of review checkpoints before the final workshop delivery.
- Include more career guidance activities, mock interviews, or experience-sharing sessions near the end of the program.
- Continue maintaining the FCAJ community after the internship so interns can keep collaborating and sharing resources.

I sincerely appreciate what the First Cloud AI Journey program and support teams provided throughout my internship. This was a meaningful experience that strongly contributed to my academic direction and career development.
